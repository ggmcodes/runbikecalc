How to Use a Race Card

A race card combines split times with nutrition reminders into a single compact strip you carry during your race. Unlike a basic pace band that only shows times, a race card is your complete race-day reference — telling you both where you should be and what you should be consuming at each checkpoint.

Why Race Cards Work Better Than Pace Bands

Standard pace bands show split times, but athletes often struggle to remember their nutrition plan under race-day stress. A race card puts everything in one place:

  • Distance markers so you know exactly where you are
  • Cumulative goal times to stay on pace
  • Nutrition reminders so you never miss a fueling window

Race Cards for Different Sports

Cycling

Cycling race cards are sized to fit on your bike's top tube (approximately 1.5" x 6"). Apply with clear packing tape or use a top tube race number holder. Cycling nutrition is especially important — plan for 40-60g of carbs per hour on rides over 90 minutes.

Running

Running race cards are sized for your wrist (approximately 1.5" x 8"). Wrap around your forearm and secure with clear tape or a sweatband. Keep nutrition simple — gels every 30-45 minutes with water at aid stations.

Triathlon

Triathlon race cards include separate sections for swim, bike, and run. The bike section is the most critical for nutrition since that's where you consume the most calories. Attach the card to your bike top tube and reference the run splits from memory or a separate wrist card.

Nutrition Planning Tips

  • Test in training: Never try new nutrition on race day
  • Start early: Begin fueling within the first 30-45 minutes
  • Stay consistent: Small, frequent intake beats large, infrequent meals
  • Hydrate separately: Don't rely on gels alone — plan water intake too
  • Adjust for heat: Increase fluid and electrolyte intake in hot conditions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a race card and how is it different from a pace band?

A race card is an enhanced pace band that includes three columns: distance markers, cumulative goal times, and nutrition reminders. While a standard pace band only shows split times, a race card helps you manage both pacing and fueling strategy in one compact strip that fits on a bike top tube or wrist.

What size is the printable race card?

Race cards are sized for their intended sport. Cycling cards are approximately 1.5 x 6 inches to fit on a bike top tube. Running cards are approximately 1.5 x 8 inches for wearing on the wrist. Triathlon cards include separate sections for each discipline.

How should I plan nutrition for my race card?

A general guideline is to consume 30-60g of carbohydrates per hour during endurance events lasting over 90 minutes. For cycling, plan nutrition every 20-30 minutes. For running, every 30-45 minutes. Under 90 min
