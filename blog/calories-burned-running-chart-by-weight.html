Calories Burned Running Chart

By Body Weight, Distance & Pace

Updated 2026 Printable

Quick Answer: Running burns about 0.74 calories per pound of body weight per mile. A 160 lb runner burns ~118 cal/mile, ~368 in a 5K, ~736 in a 10K, and ~3,100 in a marathon. Calories per mile stay nearly constant across paces; body weight is the biggest factor. Full charts below.

How Running Calories Are Calculated

Per Mile (quick estimate)

calories ≈ 0.74 × weight (lb)

Example: 160 lb × 0.74 = 118 cal per mile

By Time (MET method)

MET × weight (kg) × hours

Example: 9.8 × 73 kg × 0.5 = 358 cal in 30 min

Why pace barely changes calories per mile: a faster pace has a higher MET (more energy per minute) but covers the mile in less time. The two effects largely cancel, so per-mile burn stays close to 0.74 cal/lb across easy-to-moderate paces. Calories per minute, however, rise sharply with speed.
