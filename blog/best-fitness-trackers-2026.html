What to Look For in a Fitness Tracker

Form Factor

Bands are most versatile for workout tracking with screens for real-time data. Rings are most discreet and comfortable for sleep but limited for active workout tracking. Consider what you'll actually wear 24/7—a tracker in a drawer provides no data.

Subscription vs. One-Time Purchase

WHOOP ($30/month) and Oura ($6/month) require subscriptions for full features. Calculate the multi-year cost before committing. If you'll use advanced coaching features daily, subscriptions can be worthwhile. For basic tracking, one-time purchase devices offer better long-term value.

Sleep Tracking Accuracy

All trackers provide sleep duration, but accuracy of sleep staging (REM, deep, light) varies significantly. Oura Ring and WHOOP lead in accuracy; budget trackers provide rough estimates useful for trend tracking but not detailed analysis.

Recovery and Readiness Features

Recovery scores synthesize HRV, sleep quality, and other metrics to predict daily readiness. WHOOP and Oura excel here; Garmin's Body Battery is excellent; Fitbit's Daily Readiness Score requires Premium. For athletes optimizing training, recovery tracking matters more than step counting.

Battery Life

Longer battery life means more consistent tracking. Devices that die overnight miss crucial sleep data. Weekly charging (7+ days) is ideal; daily charging becomes tedious. Consider your tolerance for device management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a fitness tracker worth it if I already have a smartwatch?

It depends on what you want to track. Dedicated fitness trackers like WHOOP and Oura Ring excel at 24/7 wear, sleep tracking, and recovery metrics. If your smartwatch provides the health data you need and you don't mind charging it daily, you may not need a separate tracker.

Which fitness tracker has the best sleep tracking?

The Oura Ring Gen 3 has the most accurate sleep staging in our testing, correctly identifying REM, deep, and light sleep phases. WHOOP is a close second. Traditional band trackers like Fitbit and Garmin are less accurate but still useful for tracking trends.

Is a subscription fitness tracker worth the ongoing cost?

WHOOP ($30/month) and Oura ($6/month) require subscriptions for full features. If you'll use the detailed recovery recommendations, HRV analysis, and coaching features daily, the subscription can be valuable. If you just want step counting and basic sleep data, a one-time purchase tracker is better value.

How accurate is wrist-based heart rate tracking?

Modern optical heart rate sensors are accurate within 5-10 BPM at rest and during steady-state exercise. Accuracy decreases during high-intensity intervals and activities with wrist movement. For maximum accuracy during workouts, pair with a chest strap heart rate monitor.

What's the difference between HRV tracking devices?

HRV (heart rate variability) measures beat-to-beat variation and indicates recovery status. WHOOP and Oura measure HRV during sleep for consistency. Garmin measures morning HRV. The absolute number matters less than tracking your personal baseline and trends over time.
