Best Supplements for Runners & Cyclists 2026: What Actually Works

January 24, 2026 • 18 min read

The Evidence-Based Tier List

Tier 1 (Strong Evidence): Caffeine, Creatine, Beet Root/Nitrates, Sodium Bicarbonate

Tier 2 (Moderate Evidence): Beta-Alanine, Iron (if deficient), Vitamin D (if deficient)

Tier 3 (Limited/Mixed): BCAAs, Tart Cherry, Collagen, Adaptogens

Skip: Most "proprietary blends," testosterone boosters, fat burners, most pre-workouts

90% of supplements are marketing hype backed by cherry-picked studies. This guide focuses on what's actually proven to work for endurance athletes, with dosing protocols and product recommendations.

Tier 1: Proven Performance Enhancers

1. Caffeine - The Legal Performance Drug

Why It Works

  • • Performance gain: 2-4% improvement in endurance performance
  • • Mechanism: Blocks adenosine receptors, reduces perceived effort
  • • Optimal dose: 3-6mg/kg body weight, 30-60 min before exercise
  • • For a 70kg athlete: 200-400mg (roughly 2-4 cups of coffee)

3. Beet Root / Nitrates - Free Speed

The Science of Nitric Oxide

  • • Performance gain: 1-3% improvement in time trials
  • • Mechanism: Converts to nitric oxide, improves oxygen efficiency
  • • Dose: 300-600mg nitrate (roughly 500ml beet juice)
  • • Timing: 2-3 hours before exercise
  • • Best for: Events lasting 5-30 minutes (biggest benefit)

Tier 2: Situational Benefits

4. Beta-Alanine - For High-Intensity Efforts

When Beta-Alanine Helps

  • • Best for: Efforts lasting 1-10 minutes (think: 5K, criterium racing, HYROX)
  • • Mechanism: Buffers lactic acid, delays "the burn"
  • • Dose: 3-6g daily for 4+ weeks (loading required)
  • • Side effect: Harmless tingling (paresthesia) at higher doses

5. Iron - The Hidden Performance Limiter

Iron for Endurance Athletes

  • • Who needs it: Female athletes, vegetarians, heavy sweaters, high-mileage runners
  • • Get tested first: Check ferritin levels (optimal: 50-100 ng/mL for athletes)
  • • Warning: Don't supplement without blood work - excess iron is harmful
  • • Absorption tip: Take with vitamin C, avoid with coffee/tea/calcium

What to Skip

Supplements That Don't Work (Despite Marketing)

  • • BCAAs: If you eat adequate protein, BCAAs add nothing. Waste of money.
  • • Testosterone Boosters: None work. If you have low T, see a doctor.
  • • Fat Burners: Caffeine is the only ingredient that does anything - just take caffeine.
  • • Most Pre-Workouts: Proprietary blends with under-dosed ingredients. Get caffeine + beta-alanine separately.
  • • HMB: Minimal benefit for trained athletes.
  • • Glutamine: No benefit if protein intake is adequate.

The Priority Order

  1. 1. Sleep: 7-9 hours of quality sleep beats any supplement
  2. 2. Nutrition: Adequate protein, carbs, and whole foods first
  3. 3. Training: Consistent, smart training matters most
  4. 4. Hydration & Electrolytes: See our electrolyte guide
  5. 5. Then supplements: Caffeine, creatine, beet root for that final 2-5% edge
