Garmin vs Apple Watch for Running (2026): Which Should You Buy?

Updated June 3, 2026 · 11 min read

It's the most common question new runners ask: the Apple Watch I already love, or a dedicated Garmin? They pull in opposite directions. The Apple Watch is the best smartwatch that happens to track workouts well. Garmin makes the best training watches that happen to do some smart things. Here's how they compare for running, cycling, and triathlon — and exactly who should buy which.

Quick Verdict: Which Should You Buy?

Choose Garmin If:

  • - You train seriously and want deep data
  • - Multi-day or multi-week battery matters
  • - You do triathlon or ultra-distance events
  • - You use an Android phone (or might switch)

Choose Apple Watch If:

  • - You want one do-everything smartwatch
  • - You're an iPhone user and value the ecosystem
  • - Apps, messaging, and ECG matter to you
  • - Your events fit inside a day (Ultra 2 for long ones)

The Bottom Line

Garmin wins for dedicated training. The Forerunner 265 (~$400) gives you AMOLED looks, days of battery, and the deepest training ecosystem in the sport; step up to the 965 or fenix for multisport and week-long battery. Garmin also works with iPhone and Android.

Apple Watch wins as an everyday device. If you're on an iPhone and want apps, messages, ECG, and contactless everything on one watch that also tracks runs well, it's unbeatable — and the Ultra 2 (~$799) finally has the battery and GPS to handle marathons and long triathlons.

Our pick for most runners: Garmin if training data and battery are the priority; Apple Watch (Series for daily, Ultra 2 for endurance) if you want a smartwatch first that trains seriously second.

Battery Life: Garmin's Biggest Edge

This is the clearest difference. A Garmin Forerunner 265 runs for days between charges and 20+ hours of GPS; a fenix lasts weeks. An Apple Watch needs charging roughly daily, and even the Ultra 2 tops out around 36 hours of normal use. For most marathons either works, but for training blocks where you'd rather not charge nightly — or for ultras and multi-day events — Garmin's battery is a genuine quality-of-life and reliability advantage.

Training Data: Depth vs. Convenience

Garmin's ecosystem is built for athletes: Training Readiness, Training Status, race-time predictions, recovery time, HRV status, and structured-workout sync from platforms like TrainerRoad. The Apple Watch covers the essentials well — pace, heart rate, intervals, and a clean rings/Fitness app experience — and third-party apps add depth, but nothing matches Garmin's out-of-the-box training science. If you act on data day to day, Garmin pulls ahead; if you mostly want accurate basics with a great interface, the Apple Watch is plenty.

Ecosystem & Everyday Use

Off the run, it's no contest the other way: the Apple Watch is the best smartwatch made — apps, Messages, Apple Pay, ECG, fall detection, cellular, and seamless iPhone integration. Garmin handles notifications, music, and contactless pay, but it's a training tool first. The deciding question is often simply: do you want a smartwatch that trains, or a training watch that's a bit smart? And remember the Apple Watch only pairs with an iPhone — if you're on Android, Garmin is your answer by default.

Who Should Buy Which

Buy Garmin if you're…

A dedicated runner, cyclist, triathlete, or ultra athlete who wants the deepest data, the longest battery, and cross-platform support. Start with the Forerunner 265; go 965/fenix for multisport.

Buy Apple Watch if you're…

An iPhone user who wants one watch for everything and trains seriously but within a day's battery. Series for daily fitness; Ultra 2 for marathons, long rides, and triathlon.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Garmin or Apple Watch better for running?

For serious running and endurance training, Garmin — longer battery, deeper analytics, and iPhone + Android support. The Apple Watch is the better all-round smartwatch and trains well (especially the Ultra 2), but battery and training depth trail Garmin.

Does the Apple Watch have enough battery for a marathon?

Yes for most runners; a standard Apple Watch covers several hours of GPS, and the Ultra 2 comfortably covers a marathon or long triathlon. For ultras or multi-day events, Garmin is safer.

Which has better GPS accuracy?

Both are excellent. Apple Watch Ultra 2 and recent Garmins use multiband GPS and track within 1-2% of measured courses. GPS shouldn't be the deciding factor.

Can you use an Apple Watch with Android?

No — Apple Watch is iPhone-only. Garmin pairs with both iPhone and Android.

Which is better for triathlon?

Garmin, thanks to a purpose-built multisport mode, open-water swim metrics, and multi-day battery. The Ultra 2 can do triathlon via apps, but Garmin is the standard.
