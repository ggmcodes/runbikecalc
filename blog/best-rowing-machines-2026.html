Understanding Resistance Types

Air Resistance

Examples: Concept2

Flywheel spins faster as you pull harder, providing infinite, scalable resistance. The most realistic rowing feel. Louder than other types. The competition standard.

Water Resistance

Examples: WaterRower

Paddles in a water tank create natural, smooth resistance. Pleasant swooshing sound. Requires water purification tablets. Beautiful aesthetics.

Magnetic Resistance

Examples: Hydrow

Electromagnets provide quiet, smooth resistance. Adjustable levels. Whisper-quiet for apartments. Less realistic feel than air/water.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best rowing machine for 2026?

The Concept2 RowErg is the best rowing machine for most athletes. It's the gold standard used in gyms, CrossFit boxes, and HYROX competitions worldwide. At $990, it offers unmatched durability, accurate performance tracking, and a massive online community.

What rowing machine is used in HYROX?

HYROX competitions exclusively use the Concept2 RowErg. Station 4 requires a 1,000-meter row. If you're training for HYROX, the Concept2 is the only choice.

Is Hydrow worth the money?

Hydrow is worth it if you need motivation and prefer guided workouts. The immersive on-water classes are excellent. However, you'll need the $44/month subscription. For pure performance training, the Concept2 at $990 is better value.

Air vs water vs magnetic resistance?

Air resistance (Concept2) provides the most realistic feel with infinite scaling. Water resistance (WaterRower) offers smooth, natural rowing with pleasant sound. Magnetic (Hydrow) is quietest but feels less realistic. For serious training, air or water is preferred.
