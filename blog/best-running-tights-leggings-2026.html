Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature should I wear running tights?

55°F and below for most runners. Light tights (Endurance): 50-60°F. Full tights (Peak Mission): 30-50°F. Winter thermal tights: sub-30°F. Running in tights above 60°F usually feels too warm within 1-2 miles.

Should I wear underwear with running tights?

Optional. Most running tights have an integrated brief or gusset (no underwear needed). If not, standard underwear creates lines — either wear compression underwear designed for running or go commando.

Men's vs women's running tights?

Women's tights have higher rise waists, different hip curves, and often women's-specific fit patterns. Men's tights have flatter fronts and looser hip/thigh. Wearing the wrong gender's tights usually results in chafing or poor fit at hips.

How long should running tights last?

2-3 years of regular use. Elastic waistband wears out first (loses stretch). Signs: pilling on inner thighs, loose waistband, fabric thinning at knees. Quality tights like Patagonia last toward 3 years.
