Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a cycling-specific rain jacket?

If you ride more than twice a week in rainy seasons, yes. Cycling-specific jackets have longer sleeves to cover reaching arms, a dropped tail for bent-over positions, rear pocket access through storm flaps, and aerodynamic cuts that don't flap at speed. A hiking rain shell will work but won't stay sealed at cycling speeds and will look like a parachute.

GORE-TEX vs. proprietary membrane — does it matter?

GORE-TEX is the benchmark for waterproof-breathability. Proprietary membranes (Assos schwEr, Showers Pass Artex, Castelli Gore Infinium variants) can match or slightly beat GORE-TEX in specific metrics. The fabric matters less than construction — seam sealing, DWR coating, fit, and venting are where cheap jackets fail.

Are these jackets totally waterproof, or water-resistant?

The Rapha Pro Team GORE-TEX, Showers Pass Refuge, Showers Pass Elite III, and Assos TACTICA are all fully waterproof (seam-sealed, 15,000mm+ column). The Castelli Gabba R is water-resistant + windproof — it's a hybrid, not meant for hours-long downpours but outstanding for cold drizzle and mist.

Pit zips worth it?

Yes, almost always. Cyclists generate a LOT of heat, and even the most breathable membrane saturates in hard efforts. Pit zips let you dump heat fast without shedding the jacket. Only skip pit zips on an ultralight race-day jacket like the Rapha Pro Team — where weight is the priority over venting.

Dress under the shell: what works?

Cold rain (40-55°F): merino base + thermal long-sleeve jersey under the shell. Mild rain (55-70°F): summer jersey + arm warmers. Warm rain (70°F+): summer jersey only. Avoid cotton anything. Pack a merino neck gaiter — keeps the collar gap closed.
