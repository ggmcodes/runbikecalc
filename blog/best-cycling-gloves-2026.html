Frequently Asked Questions

How should cycling gloves fit?

Snug without restricting blood flow. Fingers should reach the end (but not extend past). Palm pad should sit flat on your palm, not bunch up. Wrist closure should be firm but not tight.

Gel padding vs no padding?

Gel padding — reduces numbness on long rides, better for bikepacking/touring, but reduces handlebar feel. No padding — direct handlebar connection, better for racing. For most riders, light gel padding is the sweet spot.

Full-finger vs fingerless?

Fingerless — road cycling standard, ventilation, easier phone use. Full-finger — MTB standard (protects from brush and crashes), better for cold weather. Most road cyclists use fingerless; MTB riders use full-finger.

How long do cycling gloves last?

1-2 years of regular use. Palms wear out first, especially at the thumb. Signs: thinning palm material, lost grip, stretched cuffs. Quality gloves like Castelli Entrata last toward 2 years.
