Frequently Asked Questions

Prizm vs ChromaPop vs Clarity — which lens tech is best?

All three enhance contrast beyond UV-only lenses. Prizm (Oakley) — best for road (bright sun/pavement contrast). ChromaPop (Smith) — best for trail (dirt/mud/foliage contrast). Clarity (POC/Zeiss) — best overall optical clarity. Pick based on where you ride most.

Do cycling sunglasses need to be shatterproof?

Yes — high-speed crashes and flying debris (rocks, bugs at 25+ mph) hit hard enough to break regular sunglass lenses. Cycling-specific sunglasses use polycarbonate lenses that meet ANSI Z87.1 impact standards.

Photochromic vs multiple lenses?

Photochromic (auto-adjusting) — one pair covers all conditions, more convenient, 90% as good as the right dedicated lens. Multiple lenses (clear, smoke, yellow, mirrored) — better performance for specific conditions, hassle to swap mid-ride. For most cyclists, photochromic wins on practicality.

How do I prevent cycling sunglasses from fogging?

Three things: (1) buy glasses with ventilation channels (most cycling-specific brands have them), (2) anti-fog spray (Cat Crap, RainX), (3) avoid over-tight helmet strap that pushes sunglasses hard against your face.
