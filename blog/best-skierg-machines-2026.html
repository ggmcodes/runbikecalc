Frequently Asked Questions

What SkiErg is used in HYROX?

HYROX uses the Concept2 SkiErg exclusively for Station 1 (1,000m ski). If you're training for HYROX, the Concept2 is the only machine that will give you race-identical training.

Is a SkiErg worth it for home gym?

Yes, the SkiErg is worth it if you train for HYROX, want upper body cardio, or need a full-body workout machine with low impact. At $1,000 for the Concept2, it's built to last 20+ years with minimal maintenance.

Do I need a floor stand for SkiErg?

You can wall-mount the SkiErg or use a floor stand. Wall mounting saves floor space and is most stable. The floor stand ($200) is better if you can't drill into walls or want to move the machine. HYROX competitions use floor stands.

SkiErg vs rowing machine: which is better?

The SkiErg targets upper body and core more, while the rower is more leg-dominant. For HYROX, you need both‚ÄîStation 1 is ski, Station 4 is row. For general fitness, the rower offers more total-body engagement. Many athletes get both.

How much space do I need for a SkiErg?

Wall-mounted SkiErg needs about 5 feet of floor space and 8 feet of ceiling height. With floor stand, plan for 4ft x 5ft footprint. The floor stand model takes up more room but can be moved.
