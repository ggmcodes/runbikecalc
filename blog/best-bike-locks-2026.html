Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I spend on a bike lock?

Rule of thumb: 10-20% of your bike's value. $1000 bike → $100-200 lock. Commuter bike in high-theft city: spend more. Don't cheap out on locks — a $30 cable lock defeats in 30 seconds with bolt cutters.

U-lock vs chain vs folding — which is best?

U-lock — most secure for weight, limited reach. Chain — more flexible, heavier, often weaker per dollar. Folding — compact for transport, medium security. For most commuters: U-lock + folding for different parking scenarios.

How to lock a bike properly?

(1) Lock frame + rear wheel to an immovable object. (2) Put key side facing down/ground (harder to attack). (3) Fill the U with as much bike as possible (less room for tools). (4) Use a second lock (cable through front wheel) if possible. (5) Park in visible, well-lit areas.

Are angle grinders actually a problem?

Yes — they're the tool that defeats virtually every bike lock within 30-90 seconds. Standard U-locks can't resist them. Only specialized materials (Hiplok D1000's Ferosafe, Altor's ceramic) can. For most cyclists, pick a lock that's angle-grinder resistant + avoid parking in theft hot spots.
