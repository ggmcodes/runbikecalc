For DIY home fitting, the essentials are: tape measure, plumb bob, and a Park Tool MLP-1 Master Link Pliers. For serious fit adjustments: Retül Vantage Bike Fit System (pro studios) or Specialized Body Geometry Fit (dealer-installed).

A good bike fit prevents knee pain, back pain, and power loss. Professional fits run $200-500 but a DIY fit with the right tools gets you 80% of the way there. This guide covers tools for home fitting and the pro equipment fit studios use.

Frequently Asked Questions

DIY fit vs professional fit?

DIY — saves $200-500, works for 70% of fit needs. Use online calculators, saddle level, and app-based analysis. Professional fit — worth it if you have persistent pain, race seriously, or just bought a new bike. Retül + Specialized BG fits are the gold standard.

What saddle height should I run?

LeMond formula: inseam × 0.883 = saddle height from BB center to top of saddle. Holmes method: heel on pedal at 6 o'clock, leg should be straight. Start here, adjust ± 5mm based on knee comfort and pedaling feel. See our frame size calculator.

How often should I get a bike fit?

When you buy a new bike. After significant fitness changes (gain/lose 20+ lbs). After 3-6 months of consistent riding (fit needs may shift as flexibility changes). After switching from road to gravel (different position). Most cyclists fit once per bike.

Can an AI app replace a pro fitter?

For 70-80% of cases, yes. AI apps like MyVeloFit analyze knee/hip angles from video and recommend specific adjustments. Not as accurate as Retül 3D motion capture but close enough for most riders at 10% of the cost.
