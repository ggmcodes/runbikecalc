Frequently Asked Questions

Can I do my own bike fit at home?

Yes, with the right tools you can address many common fit issues. Cleat wedges, saddle adjustments, and stem changes can be done DIY. For complex issues or chronic pain, a professional fit is recommended.

What are cleat wedges and do I need them?

Cleat wedges go between your shoe and cleat to correct natural foot cant (tilt). Many cyclists have feet that don't sit flat—this causes knee pain and inefficient pedaling. They're the most cost-effective bike fit improvement you can make.

How do I know if I need cleat wedges?

Common signs: knee tracking inward or outward during pedal stroke, medial or lateral knee pain, hot spots on ball of foot, or feeling unstable on the pedals. Most people benefit from some degree of wedging.
