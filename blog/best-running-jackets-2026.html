Frequently Asked Questions

Wind jacket vs rain jacket — which do I need?

For most runs, a wind jacket (Houdini) handles 80% of conditions — blocks wind, sheds light rain, packable. Add a rain jacket (Storm Racer) for actual rain events or if you run in wet climates. Many runners own both.

How waterproof do I need my running jacket?

Gore-Tex / Pertex — highest waterproofing, most breathable, most expensive. DryVent / H2No — mid-tier, still fully waterproof, more affordable. Water-resistant (DWR only) — sheds light rain but soaks in heavy rain. For most runners, mid-tier waterproofing is the sweet spot.

Can I use a hiking jacket for running?

Technically yes — but hiking jackets are usually heavier, less packable, and cut for backpacks rather than running motion. Running-specific jackets are lighter, stuff smaller, and have better breathability under exertion.

How should a running jacket fit?

Slim enough that it doesn't flap at speed, loose enough for full arm swing. Layer over a long-sleeve shirt for shoulder season, over a base layer only for warm rain. Check cuff elasticity — loose cuffs let water run up your sleeves.
