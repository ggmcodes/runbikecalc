Frequently Asked Questions

Whey vs casein vs plant protein?

Whey — fastest digestion (20-30 min), best for post-workout. Casein — slow digestion (6-8hr), best pre-bed. Plant (pea+rice+hemp) — slower than whey, comparable amino profile, better for digestion issues or vegans. For most endurance athletes, whey for post-workout + plant for pre-bed works well.

How much protein do endurance athletes need?

1.2-1.6g per kg bodyweight daily. 70kg runner = 85-115g/day. From whole foods first (meat, eggs, dairy, legumes). Protein powder fills gaps — one shake = 20-30g, covers ~25% of daily target for most athletes.

Do endurance athletes need different protein than bodybuilders?

Slightly. Bodybuilders: 2g/kg, all about muscle growth. Endurance: 1.2-1.6g/kg, focused on recovery + immune support. Skratch Recovery's 4:1 carb:protein ratio is actually better for endurance than pure-protein shakes (which bodybuilders prefer).

When should I take protein?

Post-workout (within 2 hours) — primary recovery window. Pre-bed (casein) — overnight recovery. Split remaining protein across meals. Pre-workout protein doesn't help performance much for most endurance athletes.
