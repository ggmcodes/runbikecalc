Race Cut vs Club Cut Fit

Race cut (Castelli, Rapha Pro Team, Assos EQUIPE) = skin-tight, aerodynamic, longer in back to cover your drops posture. Size up one if you're between sizes or want a less aggressive look.

Classic/club cut (Rapha Classic, PEARL iZUMi relaxed) = half-size more forgiving, reads well off the bike. Less aerodynamic but more comfortable at coffee stops.

For sustained hot-weather riding, race cut actually performs better — less fabric volume means faster evaporative cooling.

What Makes a Good Cycling Jersey

  • Fabric weight. Below ~130g/m² for summer. Mesh or laser-cut perforations on back/sides are bonus.
  • UPF rating. Look for UPF 30+ — cycling puts you in direct sun for hours.
  • Pocket structure. Three rear pockets with a zipped security pocket is the minimum. Reinforced bottoms if you carry heavy tools.
  • Silicone hem gripper. Non-negotiable for race-cut jerseys — prevents ride-up on descents.
  • YKK full zip. Quality zippers last years. Cheap zippers fail.

Frequently Asked Questions

How should a cycling jersey fit?

Race-cut jerseys should fit snugly without restricting breathing. Sleeves end just above the elbow (biceps). Hem should reach the bottom of your back pockets when standing. If you're between sizes, size up — a tight jersey in a riding position feels even tighter.

Castelli vs Rapha — which is better?

Castelli (Italian) — broader range, aggressive race-cut fits, best value at pro level. Rapha (British) — premium pricing, cleaner aesthetic, strong brand identity. For performance per dollar, Castelli wins. For brand loyalty and long-term durability, Rapha.

How long do cycling jerseys last?

Quality jerseys last 3-5 years of regular use. Washing in cold water and hanging to dry extends life significantly. Common failure points: elastic hem grippers (wear out first), zipper pulls, pocket seams.

Do I need women's-specific jerseys?

Yes — women's cycling jerseys have different shoulder slope, bust room, and torso length. All major brands (Castelli, Rapha, Assos, PEARL iZUMi) offer women's-specific versions of their main models.
