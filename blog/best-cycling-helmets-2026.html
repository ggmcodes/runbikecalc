Frequently Asked Questions

MIPS vs WaveCel vs SPIN — which rotational impact tech is best?

All three work — MIPS (most common, well-tested) uses a sliding liner. WaveCel (Bontrager/Trek) uses a collapsing cellular structure. SPIN (POC) uses silicone pads. Independent testing shows similar performance across all three. Pick based on fit, not brand.

How often should I replace a cycling helmet?

Every 3-5 years even without a crash — UV and sweat degrade foam over time. Always replace immediately after a crash impact, even if there's no visible damage. Foam is single-use for major impacts.

Do I need a specific MTB helmet or will a road helmet work?

Road helmets lack the extended rear coverage that MTB helmets have (important for back-of-head crashes on trails). For serious MTB, get a trail helmet (Smith Forefront, Giro Montaro). For casual commute or light gravel, road helmets are fine.

How should a cycling helmet fit?

Snug around the entire head without pressure points. Two fingers between your eyebrows and the helmet front. Straps form a V just below the ears. Should not move when you shake your head side to side.
