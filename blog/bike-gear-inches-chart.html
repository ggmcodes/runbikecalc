Bike Gear Inches Chart

Every Chainring & Cog Combination for 700c Wheels

Updated 2026 Printable

Quick Answer: Gear inches = wheel diameter (in.) × (chainring teeth ÷ cog teeth). A 700c wheel is ~26.4″ in diameter, so a 50×11 gives ~120 gear inches (hard/fast) and a 34×32 gives ~28 gear inches (easy climbing). Higher = harder gear; lower = easier. Full lookup table below.

What Do the Numbers Mean?

20–40 in

Climbing gears. For steep grades, loaded touring, and low-cadence ascents.

50–70 in

Cruising gears. Comfortable spinning on flats and rolling terrain.

75–95 in

Tempo and fast-group gears. Holding speed on flats with a tailwind.

100–125 in

Sprinting and descending. The biggest gears most riders ever use.

Gear Inches Formula

Gear Inches

wheel dia (in) × (chainring ÷ cog)

Example: 26.4 × (50 ÷ 11) = 120 gear inches

Development (meters)

gear inches × 0.0798

Distance traveled per pedal revolution. 120 in ≈ 9.6 m.

Wheel diameter reference: 700c × 23mm ≈ 26.3″, 700c × 25mm ≈ 26.4″, 700c × 28mm ≈ 26.6″, 700c × 32mm ≈ 26.9″. For exact tire-specific values, use the gearing calculator below.
