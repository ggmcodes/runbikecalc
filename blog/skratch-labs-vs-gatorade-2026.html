Skratch Labs vs Gatorade (2026): Which Electrolyte Drink Is Better?

Updated June 3, 2026 · 8 min read

Gatorade defined the sports drink. Skratch Labs was built to fix what endurance athletes don't like about it — too much sugar, not enough sodium, and a list of artificial ingredients. So which actually belongs in your bottle? It comes down to how long you sweat and what your gut tolerates. Here's the honest breakdown.

Quick answer: For long, hot, or hard endurance sessions, Skratch Labs wins — more sodium, less sugar, real ingredients, easier on the stomach. For short workouts, team sports, and budget, Gatorade is perfectly good and far cheaper.

Sodium: The Stat That Matters Most

Sodium is the electrolyte you lose most in sweat, and it's the one that prevents cramping and hyponatremia on long days. Skratch's ~380 mg per serving is meaningfully closer to what most athletes need during extended exercise than regular Gatorade's ~270 mg. If you're a salty sweater or training in heat, that gap matters — and it's why endurance athletes gravitate to Skratch (or reach for Gatorade's higher-sodium Endurance Formula). Sugar & Gut Comfort

Regular Gatorade carries roughly twice the sugar of Skratch. On a short, hard effort that sugar is usable fuel — no problem. But over several hours, a sweeter, higher-osmolality drink sits heavier and can contribute to GI distress. Skratch was specifically formulated at a lighter concentration to empty from the stomach quickly, which is why many ultra and long-course athletes find they can drink it for hours without their stomach turning.

Ingredients & Taste

Skratch uses cane sugar and real freeze-dried fruit for a light, natural flavor with no artificial colors — many describe it as "barely flavored," which is the point on a long ride. Gatorade is bolder, sweeter, and artificially colored, which some people prefer for palatability. Neither is "unhealthy" in context; it's a question of clean-and-light vs. sweet-and-familiar.

The Verdict

Choose Skratch Labs if…

You ride or run long and hot, you're a salty sweater, you want clean ingredients, or Gatorade's sweetness bothers your stomach over time. It's the better endurance drink, full stop — you just pay for it.

Choose Gatorade if…

You want maximum value and availability, you're doing shorter or team-sport efforts, or you like a sweeter taste. For long days, step up to Gatorade Endurance Formula to close the sodium gap.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Skratch Labs better than Gatorade?

For endurance athletes, generally yes — more sodium, less sugar, real ingredients, and easier on the gut over hours. Gatorade is cheaper and more available, which makes it fine for short sessions and team sports.

Does Skratch or Gatorade have more sodium?

Skratch — about 380 mg per serving vs ~270 mg in 20 oz of regular Gatorade. Gatorade's separate Endurance Formula is higher (~620 mg).

Why is Skratch more expensive?

Real cane sugar and real fruit instead of artificial colors/flavors, sold as a specialty endurance product in smaller volumes. You pay more per serving for cleaner ingredients and a better sodium-to-sugar ratio.

Is Gatorade bad for endurance athletes?

No — it's better than water for efforts over an hour. The drawbacks for long endurance use are lower sodium, higher sugar, and artificial additives. For long days, choose a higher-sodium option.

Best Gatorade alternative for runners and cyclists?

Skratch Labs is the most popular clean alternative, with LMNT, Nuun, and Precision Hydration as strong options depending on how much sodium and carbohydrate you want.
