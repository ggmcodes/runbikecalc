Triathlon-Specific Features Explained

Multisport Mode

One-button transition between swim, bike, run. Tracks T1/T2 times separately. All watches above support this.

Open Water Swim

Uses GPS to track distance, pace, and stroke rate in lakes/ocean. Critical for Ironman swims where pool-based estimates fail.

Power Meter Support

Connect to cycling power meters for watt-based training and racing. Essential for pacing the bike leg.

Race Predictor

Garmin's race predictor estimates finish times for different distances. Helpful for goal setting and pacing strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best triathlon watch for 2026?

The Garmin Forerunner 965 is the best triathlon watch for most athletes. It offers exceptional GPS accuracy, AMOLED display, open water swim metrics, cycling dynamics, and 31-hour GPS battery life‚Äîall for $599.

Can I use my triathlon watch in the pool and open water?

Yes, all watches in this guide are water-resistant to at least 50 meters and track both pool and open water swimming. Open water mode uses GPS to track distance and stroke data.

Do I need a triathlon watch for sprint triathlon?

For sprint and Olympic distance, any GPS running watch with basic swim tracking works fine. Triathlon-specific watches become more valuable for 70.3 and Ironman where detailed pacing, nutrition alerts, and long battery life matter.

COROS vs Garmin for triathlon: which is better?

Garmin has more triathlon-specific features and larger ecosystem. COROS offers better battery life and value. For serious Ironman athletes, Garmin's training load, recovery metrics, and race predictor are hard to beat. COROS is excellent for budget-conscious triathletes.
