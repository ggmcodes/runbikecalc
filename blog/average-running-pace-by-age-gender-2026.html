Average Running Pace by Age and Gender: Complete Data Chart (2026)

Comprehensive tables of average mile times, 5K, 10K, half marathon, and full marathon finish times for every age group — so you know exactly where you stand

By Glen • Updated 2026 • 10 min read

The Direct Answer

The average running pace for an adult male is 9:03 per mile. For an adult female it is 10:21 per mile. These figures represent median road race finishers across all adult age groups. Pace declines roughly 1% per year after age 35, and men run approximately 10-15% faster than women at equivalent training levels.

Use the tables below to find the average for your specific age group and gender, then compare to your own times.

How Much Does Running Pace Decline With Age?

Based on longitudinal race data, running performance declines at a predictable rate. Here is the expected annual pace decline by decade.

Age Decade Annual Pace Decline Cumulative Effect Per Decade Primary Cause
20s–30s ~0.5% / year ~5% slower Minor VO2 max decline, lifestyle changes
30s–40s ~0.8% / year ~8% slower VO2 max decline, slower recovery
40s–50s ~1.0% / year ~10% slower Muscle mass loss, reduced stride length
50s–60s ~1.5% / year ~15% slower Significant VO2 max and lactate threshold drop
60s–70s ~2.0% / year ~20% slower Significant muscle loss, reduced neuromuscular efficiency

Well-trained masters runners who maintain high training volume experience slower decline — often 0.5-0.7% per year through their 50s. Resistance training and speed work are the most effective tools to slow age-related pace decline.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average running pace for a 40-year-old?

The average running pace for a 40-44 year old male is approximately 9:02 per mile. For women in the same age group, the average is around 10:21 per mile. These figures represent median road race finishers, not elite or beginner performance.

What is a good mile time by age?

A good mile time varies by age. For men ages 20-24, running a mile under 7:30 is good. For men 40-44, under 8:30 is considered above average. For women ages 20-24, under 9:00 is good. For women 40-44, under 9:45 is a solid performance that places you in the top 25% for your age group.

Does running pace decline with age?

Yes, but the decline is gradual. Most runners maintain their best times into their mid-30s, then experience roughly a 1% decline per year through their 40s and 50s. After 60, the decline accelerates to about 1.5-2% per year. Consistent training, strength work, and speed workouts are proven ways to slow this decline.

How do I know if I have a good pace for my age?

Compare your pace to the median in the tables above. If you are faster than the listed average for your age and gender, you are above the median. For a precise ranking, use our Age-Graded Calculator, which converts your time into a percentage of the world-record pace for your exact age. A score above 50% is average; above 60% is competitive; above 70% is excellent.

What is the average marathon finish time?

The overall average marathon finish time across all adult ages is approximately 4:32 for men and 4:59 for women. For the 35-39 age group (peak participation decade), the average is 4:29 for men and 4:57 for women. The most common marathon finish time is between 4:00 and 5:00 for both genders.
