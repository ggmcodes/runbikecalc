Why Amsterdam for Your PR

If you're serious about running a personal best marathon, the TCS Amsterdam Marathon should be at the top of your list. This is one of the fastest marathon courses in the world - a pancake-flat, 26.2-mile journey through one of Europe's most beautiful cities with conditions optimized for speed.

The numbers speak for themselves: Amsterdam has produced numerous national records and thousands of personal bests. The course has only about 50 feet of total elevation gain - flatter than even Chicago or Berlin. The smooth Dutch roads, cool October weather, and excellent organization create ideal conditions for fast times at every level.

First held in 1975, the Amsterdam Marathon has grown into one of Europe's premier races, attracting approximately 20,000 marathon runners from over 100 countries. The course takes you through Amsterdam's UNESCO World Heritage canal district, past the famous Rijksmuseum, along the scenic Amstel River, through the beautiful Amsterdamse Bos forest, and finishes inside the historic 1928 Olympic Stadium - where runners enter through the original marathon tunnel onto the track for a truly unforgettable finish.

The 2026 Amsterdam Marathon: Scheduled for Sunday, October 18, 2026. Registration opens in early 2026. The race is popular and sells out, so register early to secure your spot on one of the world's fastest marathon courses.

Course Profile: The Flat Truth

The Amsterdam Marathon is a loop course that starts and finishes at the Olympic Stadium. The course winds through Amsterdam's city center, along the Amstel River, and through the Amsterdamse Bos forest before returning to the stadium. It is remarkably, almost impossibly flat.

Key Course Statistics

Start/Finish Olympic Stadium (Olympisch Stadion)
Total Elevation Gain ~50 feet (15 meters) - Exceptionally flat
Maximum Elevation ~10 feet above sea level
Course Type Loop course, pancake flat
Course Record (Men) 2:03:34 (Tamirat Tola, 2021)
Course Record (Women) 2:17:57 (Almaz Ayana, 2022)
Time Limit 6 hours
Field Size ~20,000 marathon runners

Mile-by-Mile Course Breakdown

Miles 1-3: Olympic Stadium & City Center

Start inside the historic Olympic Stadium and exit through the marathon tunnel. Head into Amsterdam's city center. The streets are wide and flat. Crowds are enthusiastic. Run controlled - don't get caught up in the excitement and go out too fast.

Miles 3-7: Canal Belt & Rijksmuseum

Run through Amsterdam's famous canal district (UNESCO World Heritage Site). Pass the magnificent Rijksmuseum - one of the world's great art museums. The historic architecture and canal bridges provide stunning scenery. Flat as a pancake.

Miles 7-10: Amstel River

Head south along the scenic Amstel River. Beautiful views of historic buildings and houseboats. This section is serene and flat. Good crowd support along the river. Find your rhythm here.

Miles 10-15: South Amsterdam & Approach to Forest

Continue through southern Amsterdam neighborhoods. Pass traditional Dutch windmills (De Rieker windmill). Approach the Amsterdamse Bos forest. The halfway point comes around mile 13. Check your split - adjust if needed.

Miles 15-22: Amsterdamse Bos (THE FOREST SECTION)

Run through the beautiful Amsterdamse Bos (Amsterdam Forest) - a massive park with tree-lined paths. This section is peaceful with thinner crowds but beautiful scenery. Stay mentally focused. The trees provide wind shelter. Still flat!

Miles 22-25: Return to City

Exit the forest and head back toward the Olympic Stadium. Crowds build as you approach the city. Energy increases. The finish is close - dig deep for these crucial miles.

Miles 25-26.2: Olympic Stadium Finish

Approach the historic Olympic Stadium. Enter through the famous marathon tunnel. Emerge onto the track where the 1928 Olympics were held. Run a final lap with massive crowds cheering. Cross the finish line on the Olympic track - an unforgettable moment.

The Amsterdam Advantage: With only 50 feet of total elevation gain, Amsterdam is flatter than Berlin, Chicago, or any other major marathon. The course is specifically designed for fast times. If you're fit and ready, this is where PRs happen.

Amsterdam-Specific Training

Training for Amsterdam is all about flat course pacing and speed. The lack of hills means your training should focus on maintaining consistent pace for 26.2 miles.

Key Training Focuses

Even Pace Mastery

The flat course rewards metronomic pacing. Practice running exact splits for extended periods. Do long runs where every mile is within 5 seconds of goal pace.

Marathon Pace Endurance

Amsterdam's flat profile allows for aggressive marathon pace work. Build to 14-16 miles at goal marathon pace in your peak training phase.

Mental Preparation for Forest Section

The Amsterdamse Bos section (miles 15-22) has thinner crowds. Practice staying focused during quieter sections of long runs.

Wind Preparation

Dutch weather can include wind. Train on windy days when possible. Practice maintaining rhythm in headwinds.

16-Week Training Overview

Weeks 1-4: Base Building

  • Build to 45-55 miles/week
  • Focus on consistent easy pace running
  • One tempo run per week (20-30 min)
  • Long runs building to 14-16 miles

Weeks 5-8: Strength & Speed

  • Peak mileage: 55-70 miles/week
  • Marathon pace long runs: 16-18 miles with 10-12 at goal pace
  • Tempo runs extending to 45-60 minutes
  • Even-split practice: 12+ miles at exact goal pace

Weeks 9-12: Race-Specific Phase (KEY WEEKS)

  • Long runs: 20-22 miles with 14-16 at marathon pace
  • Even-split long runs: 18+ miles at exact goal pace
  • Tune-up race (half marathon recommended)
  • Practice race nutrition strategy

Weeks 13-16: Taper & Travel Prep

  • Reduce mileage: 70% > 50% > 30%
  • Maintain some intensity
  • Final long run: 12-14 miles (2 weeks out)
  • Travel to Amsterdam 2-3 days before race

Netherlands Travel Planning

Amsterdam is one of Europe's most accessible and tourist-friendly cities. Compact, walkable, and with excellent public transportation, it's easy to navigate for marathon weekend.

When to Arrive

Arrive in Amsterdam 2-3 days before the race. The time difference from North America is 6-9 hours, so some jet lag adjustment is needed.

Where to Stay

  • Near Olympic Stadium: Walking distance to start/finish, quieter area
  • Museum District/Vondelpark: Central, near course, upscale options
  • City Center: More options, but farther from start
  • Book early: Marathon weekend hotels fill quickly

Getting Around Amsterdam

  • Trams: Excellent coverage, Tram 24 goes to Olympic Stadium
  • Metro: Fast for longer distances
  • Walking/Biking: Amsterdam is very walkable, bikes everywhere
  • From Schiphol Airport: Direct train to Amsterdam Centraal (~15 min)

Pre-Race Dining Tips

  • Dutch cuisine includes excellent breads and pastries for carb-loading
  • Italian restaurants are plentiful - stick to simple pasta dishes
  • Indonesian cuisine is popular in Amsterdam - rice dishes work well
  • Avoid heavy Dutch specialties (stamppot, bitterballen) close to race day

Weather Preparation

October weather in the Netherlands is typically ideal for marathoning - cool temperatures that allow you to push hard without overheating. However, Dutch weather can be unpredictable.

Dutch Weather Reality: The Netherlands is known for unpredictable weather. The famous saying "four seasons in one day" applies. Prepare for anything from perfect conditions to rain and wind. The good news: cool temps are almost guaranteed, which is ideal for fast times.

PR Pacing Strategy

Amsterdam's flat profile is perfect for aggressive, even pacing. The key to your PR is discipline - run the same pace from mile 1 to mile 26.

The Even Split PR Strategy

The Goal: Run identical splits for every mile. On this flat course, negative splits aren't necessary - even splits produce the fastest times. The course gives you nothing to hide behind and nothing to blame. It's pure running.

Race Week in Amsterdam

Expo & Packet Pickup

The Amsterdam Marathon Expo is held at the Olympic Stadium, Friday and Saturday before the race.

  • Bring passport/ID and race confirmation
  • The expo is at the start/finish venue - easy to preview
  • Go Friday to avoid Saturday crowds
  • Tram 24 stops near the stadium

Race Morning Timeline

  • 6:00-6:30 AM: Wake up, eat pre-race meal
  • 7:30-8:00 AM: Leave for Olympic Stadium
  • 8:00-9:00 AM: Arrive, drop bags, use restrooms
  • 9:00-9:20 AM: Move to starting pens
  • 9:30 AM: Marathon start

Race Day Execution

Key Race Moments

Mile 1-7: City & Canals

Exit the stadium through the marathon tunnel. Stay controlled through the city and canal district. Crowds are great. Don't get pulled too fast by the excitement.

Mile 7-14: Amstel River

Beautiful riverside running. Lock into your goal pace. This is where you build the foundation for your PR.

Mile 15-22: Forest Focus

The Amsterdamse Bos section. Crowds thin but scenery is beautiful. Stay mentally engaged. Maintain your pace. The trees provide wind shelter.

Mile 23-26.2: Olympic Finish

Crowds build as you approach the stadium. Enter through the historic marathon tunnel. Emerge onto the Olympic track. The roar of the crowd. Your PR moment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Amsterdam Marathon course like?

The TCS Amsterdam Marathon is one of the flattest marathons in the world. The course starts and finishes at the 1928 Olympic Stadium, running through Amsterdam's canal district, past the Rijksmuseum, along the Amstel River, and through the Amsterdamse Bos forest. With only about 50 feet of total elevation gain, it's an ideal PR course.

When is the Amsterdam Marathon 2026?

The TCS Amsterdam Marathon 2026 is scheduled for Sunday, October 18, 2026. The race is typically held on the third Sunday of October. The marathon start is at 9:30 AM from the Olympic Stadium.

How do I register for the Amsterdam Marathon?

The Amsterdam Marathon uses open registration that typically opens in late winter/early spring. There's no lottery - spots are first-come, first-served. Entry fee is approximately 85-120 euros. The race typically sells out several months before race day.

Why is Amsterdam considered a fast marathon course?

Amsterdam is one of Europe's fastest courses because it's exceptionally flat (only ~50 feet total elevation gain), the roads are smooth, October weather is typically cool (45-55°F), and wind protection from buildings and trees helps. The course has produced numerous national records and personal bests.

What is the weather like for the Amsterdam Marathon in October?

October weather in Amsterdam is typically ideal for marathoning - temperatures usually range from 45-55°F (7-13°C). However, Dutch weather can be unpredictable - rain is possible and some exposed sections can be windy. The cool temperatures allow you to push hard without overheating.

What landmarks will I pass during the Amsterdam Marathon?

You'll pass the 1928 Olympic Stadium (start/finish), the UNESCO World Heritage canal belt, Rijksmuseum, Vondelpark, the Amstel River, traditional Dutch windmills, and the Amsterdamse Bos forest. The Olympic Stadium finish, running through the historic tunnel onto the original track, is unforgettable.

What gear should I bring for the Amsterdam Marathon?

Pack for variable Dutch autumn weather: racing singlet and shorts, arm warmers (essential), light gloves, a packable rain jacket, and throwaway layers for the start. The smooth, flat roads are perfect for carbon-plated racing shoes.

Is the Amsterdam Marathon good for a PR attempt?

Yes, Amsterdam is one of the best marathons in the world for PR attempts. The extremely flat course, smooth roads, cool October weather, and excellent organization create ideal conditions. Many runners achieve significant personal bests here. If you're ready to chase a PR, Amsterdam should be at the top of your list.

How do I get around Amsterdam for the marathon?

Amsterdam has excellent public transportation. Trams, metro, and buses cover the city efficiently. Tram 24 goes to the Olympic Stadium. From Schiphol Airport, direct trains to Amsterdam Centraal take about 15 minutes. Consider staying near Vondelpark or the museum district for easy access to the start.
