What to Avoid for Plantar Fasciitis

  • Zero-drop or minimalist shoes (Altra, Vibram FiveFingers) — maximize plantar fascia stretch
  • Racing flats (carbon-plated 4-6mm drop) — too much heel impact
  • Underbuilt budget shoes (under $80 typically) — inadequate cushioning
  • Worn-out shoes over 400 miles — foam loses protective effect
  • Casual shoes for runs — walking shoes lack structure for impact

Stack with These Recovery Tools

  • Plantar fasciitis insoles — Superfeet Green or Powerstep Pinnacle ($40-$60). Drop them in any shoe to add arch support.
  • Calf foam roller — daily 10-min calf roll dramatically reduces PF tension. Tight calves cause PF.
  • Massage gun — 5 min on calves and arches before runs, post-run too
  • Night splint — keeps foot dorsiflexed overnight, reduces morning pain
  • Frozen water bottle — roll under arch for 10 min after every run

FAQ

Can shoes really help PF?

Yes — many runners see 50-70% pain reduction within 2-4 weeks of switching to PF-appropriate shoes (max cushioning, 8-12mm drop, arch support).

Best heel-to-toe drop for PF?

8-12mm drop. Avoid zero-drop and minimalist (4mm or less). Hoka Clifton (5mm) is the exception due to extreme cushioning.

Need orthotics?

Mild-moderate PF: Superfeet Green or Powerstep Pinnacle ($40-$60) usually sufficient. Severe: prescription custom orthotics ($300-$600, often insurance-covered).

How often replace?

PF runners: 250-400 miles or 6 months max (whichever comes first).

Run with PF or rest?

Mild (pain disappears in first 10 min): keep running. Moderate: cut mileage 50%. Severe: rest 1-2 weeks. Persistent 6+ weeks: see sports medicine doctor.
