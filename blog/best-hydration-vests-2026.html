Frequently Asked Questions

What size hydration vest do I need for a 5K or 10K?

For most 5K-10K distances, you don't need a vest at all — carry a handheld bottle. If you're in hot weather or racing a 10K, a minimalist 4-6L vest with a single 500ml flask is plenty.

How do I prevent chafing with a hydration vest?

Three things: (1) proper sizing — vests should fit snugly without bounce. (2) Anti-chafe balm (BodyGlide, TriSlide) on shoulders and under-arms before every run. (3) Avoid cotton underlayers — merino wool or synthetic shirts reduce friction.

Can I use a running hydration vest for cycling or hiking?

Running vests work for short casual cycling and day hikes, but they're race-cut for running — the fit may feel restrictive on a bike. For dedicated cycling, look at the cycling saddle bags guide. For hiking, the CamelBak Mule 12L doubles well.

How do I clean a hydration vest and reservoir?

Rinse after every sweaty run. Deep clean monthly — fill the reservoir with warm water + white vinegar (1:4 ratio), swish, drain, rinse. Hang reservoir upside down to dry (don't close it wet — mold). The vest itself: cold wash, air dry.
