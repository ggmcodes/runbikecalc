Ultra Distance 50+ Miles: Ultimate Direction Fastpack 30L. Serious gear capacity.

Fit and Sizing

Measure your chest circumference at the widest point. Most vests come in S/M/L sizes based on chest measurement. Women should choose women's-specific models like the Race Vesta for proper fit around the chest and shoulders.

Try the vest loaded with gear—fit changes dramatically between empty and fully loaded. Adjust all compression straps to eliminate bounce before committing to a size.

Preventing Bounce and Chafing

Load Distribution: Place heavy items close to your back. Water bottles in front pockets counterbalance rear storage.

Compression: Use all adjustment straps. The vest should feel snug but not restrictive.

Chafe Prevention: Apply BodyGlide or similar to contact points. Wear moisture-wicking base layers. Consider women's-specific cuts to avoid awkward seam placement.

Break It In: Test your vest on training runs before race day. Adjust strap positions and pocket loading based on your specific hot spots.

Bottles vs Bladders

Front Bottles (Recommended): Easy to refill at aid stations, monitor fluid levels, and clean. Ultimate Direction vests use this system.

Bladders: Better for very long unsupported sections where you need 2+ liters. Harder to clean and refill but keep weight centered on your back.

Common Questions

How much water capacity do I need?

For trail races with aid stations every 5-8 miles, 1 liter (two 500ml bottles) is usually sufficient. Ultra distances need 1.5-2L between aid stations. Road marathons can get by with less if you're comfortable refilling frequently.

Can I use a trail vest for road running?

Yes, but it's overkill. Trail vests have extra pockets and capacity you won't use on roads, adding unnecessary weight. A minimal road-specific vest like the Highland is more comfortable for pavement pounding.

How do I clean my hydration vest?

Hand wash with mild soap after every use, especially if you've sweated heavily or carried food. Bottles should be cleaned with bottle brushes and dried completely to prevent mold. Machine washing works for most vests but check manufacturer guidelines.

What's the difference between men's and women's vests?

Women's vests like the Race Vesta have narrower shoulder straps, adjusted chest strap placement to accommodate breasts, and different torso lengths. These changes eliminate chafing and improve bounce reduction for female runners.
