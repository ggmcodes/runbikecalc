Frequently Asked Questions

What size motor do I need for a treadmill?
For walking, a 2.0 CHP (continuous horsepower) motor is sufficient. For jogging, look for at least 2.5 CHP. Serious runners should aim for 3.0 CHP or higher. A stronger motor runs cooler at lower speeds, extending the treadmill's lifespan and providing smoother belt movement during speed changes and interval training.
Is a folding treadmill less durable than a non-folding one?
Modern folding treadmills have improved significantly and many are just as durable as non-folding models for their price range. However, commercial-grade non-folding treadmills still tend to offer heavier frames, larger motors, and longer deck surfaces. If space is a concern, a quality folding treadmill from a reputable brand is an excellent choice for most home users.
What treadmill deck size do I need?
A 20" x 60" deck is ideal for running. Walkers and shorter joggers can manage with 20" x 55". Taller runners (over 6') or those with a long stride should look for at least 22" x 60". A wider, longer deck provides a safer, more comfortable running surface and reduces the risk of stepping off the belt during intense workouts.
How much should I spend on a treadmill?
Budget treadmills under $500 work well for walking and light jogging. Mid-range models ($500-$1,000) offer better motors, larger decks, and incline options for regular joggers. Serious runners should invest $1,000-$2,000 for a durable machine with a powerful motor. Premium treadmills over $2,000 add features like touchscreens, decline capability, and commercial-grade construction.
