What is Zone 2 Training?

Zone 2 training is aerobic base training performed at 60-70% of your maximum heart rate. This intensity allows you to maintain a conversation while exercising and primarily uses fat as fuel. Zone 2 training is crucial for building aerobic capacity, improving mitochondrial function, and enhancing fat oxidation.

Benefits of Zone 2 Training

  • Improved Fat Oxidation: Trains your body to burn fat more efficiently
  • Enhanced Mitochondrial Function: Increases the number and efficiency of mitochondria
  • Better Endurance: Builds aerobic base for longer performances
  • Metabolic Health: Improves glucose metabolism and insulin sensitivity
  • Recovery: Low stress on the cardiovascular system

How to Use Your Zone 2 Heart Rate

Once you know your Zone 2 range, aim to keep your heart rate within this zone during base training sessions. You should be able to hold a conversation comfortably. If you can't speak in full sentences, you're likely above Zone 2.

Zone 2 Training Guidelines

  • Start with 20-30 minute sessions if you're new to Zone 2 training
  • Gradually increase duration to 45-90 minutes as fitness improves
  • Include 2-4 Zone 2 sessions per week in your training plan
  • Use a heart rate monitor to stay within your target zone
  • Focus on nasal breathing when possible

Calculation Methods Explained

  • Basic (220 - Age): Simple but less accurate for individuals
  • Karvonen: More accurate, uses heart rate reserve (requires resting HR)
  • Tanaka: Better for older adults and trained athletes
  • Gelish: Alternative formula with similar accuracy to Tanaka
