Lactate Threshold & Race Pace

What is LT Pace?

The fastest pace you can sustain for 45-60 minutes with steady lactate levels. Corresponds to ~15K-half marathon race effort.

Why It Predicts Performance

LT pace reflects your metabolic efficiency and aerobic capacity. Faster race paces add anaerobic contribution, slower paces require more endurance.

Accuracy Factors

Experience level, training background, race distance specialization, and environmental conditions all affect prediction accuracy.

Typical Pace Relationships

5K Pace LT - 25-35 sec/mile
10K Pace LT - 10-15 sec/mile
15K-Half Pace ≈ LT Pace
Marathon Pace LT + 15-30 sec/mile

Note: Relationships vary by individual. Elite runners have smaller gaps between paces than recreational runners.
